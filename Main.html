About

Cybersecurity is essential in protecting sensitive information, ensuring privacy, and maint aining trust in the digital world. It safeguards individuals, businesses, and governments from cyb er threats like hacking, data breaches, and malware attacks. As reliance on technology grows, cybersecurity becomes critical to preventing financial loss, protecting intellectual property, and ensuring the smooth operation of essential services. It also helps maintain the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information, which is vital for decision-making and business continuity. With evolving cyber threats, robust cybersecurity measures are necessary to minimize risks, enhance resilience, and promote a safe, secure digital environment for everyone.

Threats

Some of the common threats to cyber security are:
  • Malware: Malware refers to malicious software such as viruses, worms, ransomware, spyware, and Trojans. It can: Damage or disrupt systems. Steal sensitive information. Provide unauthorized access to attackers.
  • Phishing: Phishing attacks involve fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information, such as passwords or credit card details, by impersonating a trustworthy entity via email, text, or websites.
  • Ransomware: Ransomware encrypts the victim’s data and demands payment for its decryption. This can disrupt business operations and cause significant financial losses.
  • Social engineering: This involves manipulating individuals into revealing confidential information. Common techniques include phishing, baiting, and pretexting.

Safety Rules

  • Update software and operating systems regularly to patch vulnerabilities.
  • Create strong and unique passwords for each online account.
  • Enable multi-factor authentication to enhance security.
  • Regularly back up critical data.
  • Use reliable antivirus and firewall protection.
